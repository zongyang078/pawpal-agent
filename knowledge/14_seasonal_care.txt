Seasonal pet care tips
general
dog,cat
During summer, never leave pets in parked cars even for a few minutes as temperatures can reach dangerous levels within 10 minutes. Provide extra water and shade during hot days. Limit exercise to early morning or evening when temperatures are cooler. Watch for signs of heatstroke including excessive panting, drooling, and lethargy. In winter, wipe paws after walks to remove salt and chemical deicers which can be toxic if licked. Consider pet-safe ice melt for your own walkways. Short-haired breeds may need a coat or sweater in cold weather. Keep antifreeze securely stored as it is extremely toxic but has a sweet taste that attracts pets.
